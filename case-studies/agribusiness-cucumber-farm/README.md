# 🥒 Case Study: Greenhouse Cucumber Farm
**Client Type:** Agribusiness — Greenhouse Technology Farm
**Product:** Fresh Cucumbers (perishable)
**Service Provided:** Commercial Sales & Buyer Acquisition
**Status:** ✅ Active Client

---

## The Problem

The farm had strong production capability — greenhouse-grown cucumbers at consistent volume — but no reliable buyers.

They were selling to whoever showed up, at whatever price they could get:

- ₦400/kg on a bad day
- ₦500–₦700/kg on average
- ₦800/kg at absolute best
- **Effective average: ~₦600/kg**

Because cucumbers are **perishable**, every unsold kilogram is lost revenue. Without committed buyers lined up before harvest, the farm was constantly under pressure to accept any offer.

**Core issues:**
- No premium buyer relationships
- No pricing power
- No forecast for revenue
- High perishability risk on every harvest cycle

---

## What Lingr Network Did

### Step 1 — Buyer Profiling
We identified the two highest-value buyer categories for a greenhouse cucumber operation:
- **Premium food businesses** (restaurants, hotels, caterers) who pay for quality and consistency
- **Resellers** who need reliable supply and will pay a premium for guaranteed volume

### Step 2 — Outreach & Negotiation
We approached and closed two stable buyers:

| Buyer | Type | Price Secured | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Restaurant | Premium End Customer | ₦1,000/kg | Twice weekly, year-round |
| Reseller | Distribution Partner | ₦1,100/kg | 2–3x weekly |

### Step 3 — Revenue Tracking System
We built a full harvest-to-revenue tracking system (Excel) to document every harvest, compare old vs new pricing, and measure the ongoing impact of the buyer relationships.
