## The Pricing Problem

Most small businesses price based on what they *think the market will accept* — which is almost always lower than what premium buyers will actually pay.

The result: they compete on price instead of value, attract unreliable buyers, and leave money on the table every single transaction.

**Lingr Network's job is to close that gap.**

---

## The Three Pricing Modes

### Mode 1 — Survival Pricing ❌
*"I'll take whatever I can get."*

- Price set by desperation, not value
- Attracts lowest-tier buyers
- No consistency, no leverage
- **Kill this as fast as possible**

### Mode 2 — Market Pricing ⚠️
*"I charge what everyone else charges."*

- Price set by competitors, not differentiation
- Stable but capped upside
- Works until a competitor undercuts
- **Acceptable short-term; not the goal**

### Mode 3 — Premium Positioning ✅
*"I charge what my product is worth to the right buyer."*

- Price set by value delivered to buyer
- Attracts committed, stable buyers
- Creates pricing power and loyalty
- **This is always the target**

## How We Move Clients to Premium Pricing

### Step 1 — Find the Value Gap

Calculate the difference between current price and what premium buyers pay in the same category.

```
Value Gap = Premium Market Price − Current Client Price

Example:
Premium Market Price: ₦1,000/kg
Current Client Price: ₦600/kg
Value Gap:          +₦400/kg  (66.7% uplift opportunity)
```

### Step 2 — Identify the Premium Differentiators

What makes this product worth more to a Tier 1 buyer?

Common premium signals:
- Consistent quality (greenhouse vs open farm)
- Reliable supply (buyers hate running out)
- Clean/safe production methods
- Responsiveness and professionalism of supplier
- Flexibility (volume adjustments, delivery timing)

### Step 3 — Package and Pitch at Premium

Never lead a premium pitch with price. Lead with the differentiators, then name the price confidently.

**Wrong:** "We sell cucumbers. We charge ₦1,000/kg."
**Right:** "We supply consistent-quality greenhouse cucumbers, available twice weekly with guaranteed volume. Our price is ₦1,000/kg."

The second version justifies the price before stating it.

### Step 4 — Hold the Price

The biggest mistake after winning a premium buyer: discounting under pressure.

**Rules:**
- Do not lower the price in the first 90 days
- If a buyer pushes back, offer *more value* (extra delivery, better terms) — not a lower price
- A buyer who won't pay your price is not a premium buyer

## Price Anchoring in Negotiation

When entering any negotiation, always anchor above your target.

```
Target Price:  ₦1,000/kg
Opening Offer: ₦1,200/kg
Concession:    ₦1,000/kg (feels like a win for the buyer)
```

Buyers who negotiate down to your target price feel like they won — and are more likely to commit long-term.
